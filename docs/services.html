Service options

Four practical ways I can help

The best first project usually solves one clear bottleneck quickly: outdated website content, scattered communication, or recurring work without a simple system behind it.

For churches and faith communities

Website Updates & Announcement Support

One-time or ongoing help to keep website content, event details, and public updates clear, current, and easy to trust.

  • Homepage and key page updates
  • Service time and event information changes
  • Announcements and bulletin uploads
  • Content cleanup and consistency improvements
  • Recurring updates if needed
Best fit when important public-facing information is often outdated or updated too late.
For churches and community groups

Social Media & Public Updates Support

Practical help creating a steadier rhythm for event reminders, public updates, and ministry or program communication.

  • Announcement posts
  • Event reminder posts

  • Basic content planning
  • Caption writing and posting support
Best fit when communication is happening, but not consistently enough for people to follow.

Good first projects

Common starting points

Most engagements begin with one clear problem instead of trying to fix everything at once.

Website cleanup

Outdated information, missing updates, or inconsistent page content that needs attention.

Announcement consistency

Communication that is happening irregularly or is too hard for people to follow.

Recurring admin structure

Important work that needs simple checklists, trackers, and clearer handoffs.

Best fit when

You need practical, scoped support

  • Your team is active but stretched
  • Updates matter, but consistency is slipping
  • You want help that is clear and manageable
  • You want improvement without unnecessary complexity

Less likely to be a fit

Not a full agency model

  • Large multi-channel marketing campaigns
  • Heavy custom development projects
  • Complex branding programs
  • Big-team agency-style retainers

Next step

Start with a short conversation about what support would help most

A simple email is enough to describe your organization, what feels difficult right now, and whether you need website, communications, or operations support.
