Services

Focused support for organizations that need practical help, not complexity

Services are designed for churches, nonprofits, community organizations, and selected small local businesses that need clearer systems, more consistent updates, and support that is useful in day-to-day work.

Service options

Four practical ways I can help

The strongest first engagement is usually the one that solves a real bottleneck quickly: outdated website content, scattered communication, or recurring work without a clear system behind it.

For churches and faith communities

Website Updates & Announcement Support

Ongoing or one-time support to keep website content, event details, service information, and public-facing updates clear, current, and dependable.

  • Homepage and key page updates
  • Service time and event information changes
  • Announcements and bulletin uploads
  • Content cleanup and consistency improvements
  • Support for recurring updates
Best fit for active groups with limited time for steady digital upkeep.
For churches and community groups

Social Media & Public Updates Support

Practical help for organizations that want more consistency in event reminders, public updates, ministry or program communication, and regular posting rhythm.

  • Announcement posts
  • Event reminder posts
  • Special event promotion support
  • Basic content planning
  • Caption writing and posting support
Best fit for teams already using Facebook or Instagram and wanting more organized communication.
For nonprofits and organizations

Operations Setup & Workflow Improvement

A practical support engagement to reduce scattered work, improve follow-through, and build simple systems that are easier for real teams to maintain.

  • Recurring admin checklists
  • Action trackers and decision logs
  • Folder and file organization improvements
  • Workflow review and practical improvement suggestions
  • Light AI-enabled workflow guidance where appropriate
Best fit for teams dealing with recurring follow-through problems, scattered documents, or unclear workflows.
For selected small local businesses

Simple Website & Digital Upkeep Support

A practical option for businesses that need a simple online presence, cleaner website updates, or lighter ongoing digital support without full agency complexity.

  • Simple website setup or refresh support
  • Page content updates
  • Service and contact information cleanup
  • Basic update support after launch
  • Help keeping the website current
Best fit for businesses wanting straightforward support and a more professional digital presence.

Who these services fit best

Usually the best match for active but stretched teams

These services tend to work best when the organization already has meaningful activity, but digital upkeep, communication consistency, or recurring admin work is falling behind.

Teams with outdated or inconsistent websites

A strong fit when important public-facing information is accurate only some of the time or depends on last-minute updates.

Groups with scattered communication

A strong fit when announcements, reminders, or public updates happen irregularly and are hard for people to follow.

Organizations with recurring work but weak systems

A strong fit when important admin work relies too much on memory rather than simple checklists, trackers, and clear follow-through.

A strong first engagement often looks like

  • One clearly defined problem to solve first
  • A realistic scope instead of trying to fix everything at once
  • Simple deliverables your team can actually use
  • Clear next steps for ongoing updates if needed
  • Practical support, not unnecessary complexity

What makes this practical

Support is meant to stay clear, useful, and sustainable

Not a large-agency model

The work is focused on usefulness and real support, not broad marketing promises or unnecessary layers of complexity.

Built around actual needs

The best work happens when the problem is real, the scope is clear, and the solution is something the team can actually maintain.

Can begin small

A first engagement can be focused and limited, with ongoing support added only if it genuinely helps.
